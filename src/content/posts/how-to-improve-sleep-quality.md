Sleep well is more than just closing your eyes, it's what both your body and mind need to be at their best. But, many of us don't sleep well as stress, full days, and things that take our mind off sleep add up. 

#### **Keep a Sleep Routine**

Consistency rocks your body-clock! Also get up and go to bed at the same time on all days, weekdays or weekends. Adults generally need seven to eight hours of sleep each night. If you are one of these people who end up wide awake on your bed, looking at the roof, do not make yourself sleep. Go do something soothing for yourself like reading, listening to soft music. Go back to bed only when you are drowsy.

#### Watch What You Eat and Drink

What you eat in the evening can really effect your sleeping pattern. Large, heavy meals that are spicy or contain alcohol also tend to keep us tossing and turning at night.

#### Set the Right Sleep Environment

Cool, quiet and dark room Keep the noises down a minimum of earplugs or get a fan, Get blackout drapes. The number one thing is to not watch screens for at least an hour before bed,[ blue light signals](https://thesleephabits.com/how-your-phone-is-ruining-your-sleep/) your brain that it needs to be awake. Similarly, things that just feel nice (hot shower or gentle stretching) is helpful for your body to acknowledge you are relaxing.

#### Be Smart About Naps

Taking a quick nap during the day can give you a wake-up boost, but too much sleep, or later sleep in the day, can rob you from your nighttime sleep cycle. If you need a mid-day power nap try a short 20 - 30 minute nap in the early afternoon. If you [work night shifts naps](https://thesleephabits.com/sleep-hygiene-for-night-shift-workers-smart-habits-for-better-rest-and-energy/) can also be an effective tool during the day, if timed correctly.

#### Move Your Body

Be physically active throughout the day to help you fall asleep sooner and have more deep sleep, but not vigorous activity before bed. At night, you might consider doing light yoga or doing breathing exercises. These light evening exercises are relaxing but won't overstimulate your body. 

#### Manage Stress Before Bed

When people have trouble falling asleep the usual culprit is "racing thoughts." If you write down all the things you need to do tomorrow it helps resets the brain, then put it aside to take away the urgency. Try relaxation techniques like meditation, breathing techniques, or listening to calming music to quiet the brain. Practicing gratitude before bed will also help. 

#### Get Outdoors in Natural Light

Sunlight exposure trains your body’s natural rhythm. Try to get outside in the natural sunlight for a minimum of 20-30 minutes per day. If it is not possible to go outdoors, try to dim the lights in your home by lowering them about thirty percent each night before bed; thus allowing your body to transition to bedtime in a natural progression.

#### **Invest in Comfort**

Your mattress and pillow matter more than you know. If you wake up with aches and pains, consider upgrading. You should look for bedding that gives you support while also breathing so that your body can actually relax.

#### Supplements and Professional Help

Although some people experience good results with natural sleep aids magnesium, melatonin, you can't replace building better habits. In case you have trouble sleeping, you qual as loud snoring or not having the capacity to rest through the night time or waking up frequently at night an doctor should be visited by you and exclude conditions for example stop snoring.

Getting better sleep isn't something that happens overnight, but the choices you make each day can really add up over time. By establishing a solid routine, creating a calming environment, and managing stress effectively, you can enjoy truly restorative nights. Think of sleep as a vital necessity, it's just as crucial for your health as eating well and staying active.
